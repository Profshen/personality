NMF Analysis: 8 Traits By Demographic

        To derive the ideal personality trait theory for the given personality test dataset, I first explored different dimension reduction methods (SVD, PCA, ICA) but ultimately found that Non-Negative Matrix Factorization gave the cleanest set of personality traits. Specfically, to get those personality traits from NMF, one looks at the H matrix from the NMF. A non-negative matrix factorization decomposes the original matrix in to two matricies, a W and an H matrix. W and H approximate V but don't create a full reconstruction when multiplied because NMF's non-negative aspect makes it generally impossible to create a fully positive exact solution.



        In this case, V, the original matrix or dataset, has 50K survey respondents (rows) answering 163 questions (columns) (V = 50,000 X 163). By choosing different numbers of personality traits to pull from the data, we change the dimensions of the decomposed W and H matricies. When pulling 8 personality traits from the data, we get a W matrix with 50K survey respondents (rows) and how high they rate on the 8 personality traits (columns) (W = 50,000 X 8). The H matrix has how each of the 8 traits (rows) relates to the 163 questions (columns) (H = 8 X 163). Thus, to figure out what exact personality trait each abstract trait is actually describing, we look at the H matrix to see which personality questions most heavily weighted to a specific trait.


Choosing 8 Traits:

        When choosing the number of personality traits to pull out of the matrix, I both tried to maintain internal (within the top ten questions of trait) and external (in topics between traits) category consistency. I also aimed to pull non-trivial traits out of the dataset, meaning, I wanted the traits to capture more complex ideas around personality. After qualitatively exploring NMF models with different numbers of traits, I choose 8 because it best fit my model goals.
